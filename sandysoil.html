These soils are mainly composed of sand (greater than 70%) with very little clay (less than 15%) and/or silt. They generally hold low quantities of organic matter. The soil texture is either a sand or loamy sand. Because these soils contain very little clay or organic matter they tend to be very weakly structured. They are comparatively easy to work. No natural restructuring takes place on these soils so any compaction has to be removed using mechanical cultivations. As sand is inert with little surface bonding on particles the cation exchange capacity of these soils is very low. Due to the large particle size of sand they contain a large number of macropores which tend to contain air rather than water. Sandy soils can be worked relatively easily at any time, even in wet weather. They are normally free-draining, but some drains may be required where there is clay or other impervious material underneath, or a high water table. These soils are droughty as they have a very low available water capacity. For some crops irrigation is required.
